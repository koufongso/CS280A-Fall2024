Part 2: Fun with Frequencies

2.1: Image "Sharpening"

The Gaussian filter is a low-pass filter meaning that it only retains low frequencies of the image. Therefore, we can obtain the high frequencies of the image by subtract the blurred image from the original image. An image often looks "sharper" when it has a high frequencies components. So we can amplified the high frequcies and add them back to the original image to sharpen the image. The mathmatic expression is: I_shapr = I+a(I-(G*I)) = I+aI-aI*G = (1+a)I-aI*G = I*((1+a)*e-a*G). We can create a new "sharpening" filter (or called unsharpen mask) whcih is defined as (1+a)*e-a*G where "a" is the gain represnet that how mauch we want to amplify the high frequencies and "e" is the "unit impulse" (i.e. only has value 1 at the center of the image and 0 elsewhere).

2.2: Hybrid Images

The high frequecy tends to dominate human perception when human is close to the image but at a distance the low frequence part dominates. Based on this idea, we can blende two image into one so people can see the one image from distance and the other one when look at it from a close distance. We can use Gaussina filter to obtiain the low frequency component of an image and use impulse fitler minus the Gaussian filter (e-G) to obtian the high frequency part (since I_high_freq = I-(G*I) = I*(e-G)).

2.3: Gaussian and Laplacian Stacks

Gaussian stack is the collection of Gaussian filtered image from its previous level, starting from the orignal image (no filtered).

In Laplacian stack, image at level i is obtained by subtracting the corresponding Gaussina stack layer i+1 from Gaussina stack layer i. And to make it easier to reconstruct the orinal image (reconstruction), I save the lowest resolution layer of the Gaussian stack at the end of the Laplacian stack

Illustration of constructing Gaussian stack and Laplacian stack. Top row is the Gaussian stack and the bottom row is the Laplacian stack. Credit: CS180: Intro to Comp. Vision and Comp. Photo, Prof. Alexei Efros, UC Berkeley, Fall 2024
